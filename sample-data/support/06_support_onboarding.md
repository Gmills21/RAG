## Using the Knowledge Bot

### What is it?

The Support Knowledge Bot is your AI assistant for finding answers in our internal documentation. It helps you respond to customers faster and more accurately.

### How to use it

1. **Enter your question** in natural language
   - Example: "Can we refund an annual enterprise customer?"
   - Example: "How do I escalate an SSO issue?"

2. **Review the answer** and citations
   - Bot provides an internal answer (what you need to know)
   - Bot provides a customer-ready reply (what you can send)
   - Bot shows source documents (verify the information)

3. **Verify with source citations**
   - **ALWAYS check the citations before using an answer**
   - Click on source references to view original documents
   - If no citation is provided, DO NOT use the answer

4. **Adapt the response**
   - Customer-ready replies are templates, not final messages
   - Personalize with customer name and specific details
   - Add empathy and your own voice

### Trust but verify

**✅ GOOD: Use answers that have clear citations**
- "According to the Refund Policy (01_refund_policy.md), customers can..."
- Answer references specific section or version number
- Source document is current and official

**❌ BAD: Using answers without sources**
- Answer seems right but has no citation
- Citation is to an outdated or unofficial document
- Bot says "I don't have enough information" but you use it anyway

**Rule**: If the bot doesn't cite a source, don't trust the answer. Ask your team lead or search manually.

### When to NOT use the bot

**Don't use the bot for**:
- Customer-specific account details (use admin portal)
- Real-time system status (check status page)
- Pricing negotiations (escalate to Sales)
- Legal or compliance questions (escalate to Legal)
- Anything the bot says is unsupported or unclear

### Common mistakes new reps make

1. **Trusting answers without checking sources**
   - Always verify citations before responding to customers

2. **Copy-pasting bot responses verbatim**
   - Adapt responses to customer's specific situation
   - Add personal touch and empathy

3. **Using the bot instead of reading docs**
   - Read the actual policy documents during onboarding
   - Bot is a supplement, not a replacement for learning

4. **Not reporting wrong answers**
   - If the bot gives a wrong answer, tell your team lead
   - Help us improve the knowledge base

## Ticket Handling Best Practices

### Response Time SLAs

- **Critical (Severity 1)**: 1 hour
- **High (Severity 2)**: 4 hours  
- **Medium (Severity 3)**: 24 hours
- **Low (Severity 4)**: 48 hours

### Ticket Workflow

1. **Read the full ticket**
   - Understand customer's issue and context
   - Check their account type (Free, Pro, Enterprise)

2. **Research before responding**
   - Use Knowledge Bot for policy questions
   - Check admin portal for account details
   - Review similar past tickets

3. **Craft your response**
   - Answer their question completely
   - Anticipate follow-up questions
   - Set clear expectations for next steps

4. **Update ticket status**
   - Mark as "Waiting on Customer" if you need info
   - Mark as "Resolved" only when customer confirms
   - Add internal notes for context

### Escalation Guidelines

**Escalate to Team Lead when**:
- Policy exception required (e.g., refund after 30 days)
- Customer is angry or threatening legal action
- Issue requires Product or Engineering involvement
- You're unsure how to proceed

**Escalate to Engineering when**:
- Bug confirmed and reproducible
- Multiple customers reporting same issue
- System performance degradation
- Security concern
